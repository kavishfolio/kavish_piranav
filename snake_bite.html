SNAKE BITE

By WHO estimation 81,000 to 138,000 deaths occurred due to snake bite. The snake bites are occurred when you disturbed them or entered into their territory. In the earth only 10% to 25% of snakes have venom. There are two types of venom: Neurotoxic and Hemotoxic.

Neurotoxic

Affects the nervous system, causing paralysis.

Example: Indian Cobra, Common Krait,etc,.

Hemotoxic

Damages blood cells and tissues, causing bleeding.

Example: Saw Scaled Viper, Russell’s Viper

Cases

The major four snake bite cases found in India are Indian Cobra bite, Saw-Scaled Viper bite, Russell’s Viper bite and Common Krait bite.

Indian Cobra

Rapid Paralysis, Difficult Breathing, Blurred Vision and Sweating

Saw Scaled Viper

Intense local pain and bleeding, Spontaneous internal bleeding, Low blood pressure and shock

Russell’s Viper

Rapid swelling and bleeding, Systematic issues (Vomiting, Dizziness, Low blood pressure, Kidney failure and Neurological problems), Tissue damage and Local blistering

Common Krait

Intense local pain and bleeding, Spontaneous internal bleeding, Low blood pressure and shock

Precaution

Wear boots and long pants while entering in grassy area. Make noise while walking. Watch your place while placing your hand and step. After the snake bite stay calm and call the emergency service. The wound should be cleaned by washing with soap and water. After all the precautions the Anti-Snake Venom (ASV) should be injected to the patient. Avoid cutting the wounds, sucking venom or applying wrap tightly around the limb which stops the blood flow.

Anti Snake Venom

World Health Organization (WHO) and Ministry of Health and Family Welfare (MoHFW) strongly recommend that every hospital should have ASV in healthcare facilities as a critical component of snakebite management protocols. The ASV are made by injecting the small amount of the venom dose collected to the large animal (horse or sheep). The venom triggers the immune system of the animal and produces anti body. The plasma is harvested from their body which has more antibodies. It is processed in labs and distributed to hospitals. This is injected to the patients to neutralize the venom’s toxicity in the patient body.
